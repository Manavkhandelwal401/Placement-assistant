# The Recruiter's Playbook: Personal Conditioning & Placement Strategy
> **Author**: Your Placement Mentor
> **Target**: Mayank Khandelwal | B.Tech CSE (Tier 3) | Target: 8-12 LPA

This guide dictates your physical, mental, and tactical ruleset for the next 57 days (July 5 – September 1). No fluff. Adhere to this daily.

---

## 1. Physical & Eye Health Protocols
You cannot code effectively if your body is failing or your eyes are strained.
- **Sleep**: 7 hours minimum (12:00 AM – 7:00 AM). Do not compromise this for late-night study. Sticking to this schedule prevents cognitive decline.
- **20-20-20 Rule**: Every 20 minutes of coding, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. This prevents the eye strain that causes headaches and afternoon burnout.
- **Hydration**: Carry a 1-liter bottle in your college bag. Drink at least 3 liters of water daily. Dehydration mimics mental fatigue.
- **Posture**: Keep your screen at eye level during your 6:00 PM – 8:00 PM coding block. Do not code lying down; it induces lethargy.

---

## 2. Cognitive Conditioning & Combatting Stress
Being in a Tier 3 college means facing peer negativity, comparison, and placement anxiety.
- **The "Forget the Batch" Rule**: Your college batch rank is irrelevant. Your coding speed and conceptual clarity determine your CTC. Focus entirely on your daily logs.
- **Anti-Overload Protocol**: If you experience brain fog or loop on a coding mistake:
  1. Stop trying to trace code in your head.
  2. Draw the array/pointers on paper.
  3. Loop values manually. Externalize memory to reduce cognitive load.
- **Diaphragmatic Breathing**: If panic rises during tests or OAs, use the box breath: Inhale 4s, hold 4s, exhale 4s, hold 4s. This physiologically lowers cortisol.

---

## 3. Distraction & Disturbance Lockdown
- **Phone Protocol**: During study blocks (6:00 PM – 8:00 PM and 9:00 PM – 11:00 PM), place your phone in another room or keep it on Silent with DND active.
- **Peer Boundaries**: At college, participate in GDs and mock interviews, but ignore gossip about "which companies are visiting" or "who solved what". Work in silence.
- **YouTube Restriction**: Stop searching for general placement advice or motivation videos. Stick strictly to targeted problem tutorials (e.g., Aditya Verma's playlists).

---

## 4. Time Ingestion Hacks (Exploiting Dead Time)
- **The Commute Window (2 Hours Daily)**: Do not waste this on social media. Use your phone to:
  1. Read GFG/LeetCode editorial articles on topics you struggled with the previous night.
  2. Practice mental dry-runs of recursion trees.
  3. Study OS/DBMS key definitions.
- **The CRT Filter**: During the 9:00 AM – 4:30 PM college block, identify the low-value lectures (e.g. outdated theory slides). Use that time secretly to write pseudocode for your evening DSA problems on a physical notepad.

---

## 5. Daily DOs & DONTs Checklist

### ✅ DOs:
- **Write code from scratch**: Close reference tabs before coding. Never copy-paste.
- **Log every session**: Update `roadmap.json` daily. Tracking keeps you accountable.
- **Dry-run on paper**: Trace dry runs for recursion or tree problems before typing a single line.
- **Verify with Connectors**: Commit code to GitHub daily. Let the evidence speak.

### ❌ DONTs:
- **No Shiny Object Syndrome**: Do not spend time installing Docker, Kubernetes, or learning new frameworks until your basic arrays, strings, and DBMS concepts are locked in.
- **No Passive Watching**: Never watch a coding tutorial without coding the problem yourself.
- **No Ego-Traps**: Never spend more than 15 minutes stuck on a single coding problem during practice. If stuck, review the hints/editorial and implement.
